Statistics

Did you know that between 2010 and 2020 we lost an average of 4.7 million ha per year?

Or that over a fourth of all examined species are considered endangered?

This is a direct result of habitat loss, pollution, and climate change. These trends not only threaten wildlife but also endanger human communities that depend on forests for clean air, water, medicine, and livelihoods. By taking action now, we can reverse these trends and create a sustainable future for all.

Why is Conservation Important?

Conservation is essential for protecting biodiversity, which supports life on Earth by providing clean air, fresh water, and stable ecosystems. It helps preserve natural habitats and protects endangered species from extinction. Healthy ecosystems also play a key role in regulating the climate, preventing soil erosion, and supporting agriculture. By using natural resources responsibly, we promote sustainable development and ensure that future generations can enjoy the same environmental benefits we do today. Conservation efforts also help reduce the impact of human activities like pollution and deforestation. Every small action—from recycling to planting trees—contributes to a healthier planet and a more sustainable future.

Doing Your Part

  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle to minimize waste.
  • Use energy-efficient appliances and switch to renewable energy sources.
  • Support sustainable products and companies that prioritize environmental responsibility.
  • Plant trees and support reforestation efforts.
  • Educate others about the importance of conservation and sustainable practices.
  • Participate in local clean-up events and conservation projects.
  • Advocate for policies that protect the environment and promote sustainability.
  • Reduce water usage by fixing leaks and using water-saving fixtures.
  • Choose sustainable transportation options like walking, biking, or using public transit.
